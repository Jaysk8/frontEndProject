Space Foods

Meals In Space

Throughout history, explorers and travelers have had to devise techniques for storing food and transporting adequate sustenance for their travels. This issue was particularly severe during the period when people traveled large distances by sailing ship. Dried foods and meals preserved in salt and brine were brought by great explorers such as Columbus, Magellan, and Cook.

More recently, refrigeration and canning have provided solutions to the problem of food preservation. However, space travel required that new methods be devised for keeping foods edible. Foods taken into space must be light-weight, compact, tasty and nutritious.

During the Friendship 7 voyage in 1962, John Glenn, the first American to dine in space, ate applesauce.
